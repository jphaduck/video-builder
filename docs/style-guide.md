# Style Guide

## Creative direction (v1)
This product is for **slideshow-style YouTube storytelling**, not animation.

Default output should feel:
- cinematic
- suspenseful
- serious
- emotionally clear
- immersive and polished
- visually consistent across scenes
- optimized for long-form listening/viewing (5 to 20 minutes)

Avoid default outputs that feel overly casual, comedic, cartoonish, or flat.

## Audience framing
- Primary product user: solo faceless YouTube story creator
- End viewer: audience that watches suspense, mystery, crime, irony, and dramatic faceless storytelling videos

## Narrative style requirements
- Hook in first 15 to 30 seconds
- Clear progression of stakes and beats
- Minimal filler/repetition
- Natural transitions between scenes
- Tone consistency with selected style

## Visual direction requirements
- Still images only (with motion effects applied in timeline)
- Maintain a global style profile unless explicitly overridden per scene
- Prioritize cinematic, mood-rich interpretation over literal sentence-by-sentence illustration
- Keep images relevant to spoken story context
- Prioritize readability and subject clarity over abstract effects
- Avoid busy compositions that interfere with caption legibility
- Use auto-controlled pacing with a default target of 6 to 10 images per minute, adjusted by scene intensity

## Caption style requirements
- English captions required in v1
- Clean, high-contrast subtitle style for YouTube readability
- Caption timing should track narration accurately
- Burn-in captions are default for v1 output

## Voice and audio requirements
- Narration must remain intelligible and foregrounded
- Narration voice should feel natural, immersive, serious, and clear (not robotic)
- Delivery should support dramatic pacing where appropriate
- Music/SFX should be subtle and optional
- Default mix should favor narration clarity over dramatic loudness
- Use pronunciation overrides for names/uncommon terms as needed

## Content safety style boundaries
The style can be mature and dramatic, but output should avoid:
- explicit sexual content
- graphic gore
- hateful/extremist framing
- illegal-content framing
- obvious monetization-risk presentation for normal YouTube standards

## Editing philosophy
Enable meaningful control without overwhelming complexity:
- editable script and story beats
- editable scene prompts and image choice
- editable captions and scene timing
- editable narration/music/SFX balance
- version compare + revert for major draft artifacts

V1 should feel like a quality-first draft studio, not a full professional NLE.


## Thumbnail direction (v1)
- Thumbnail text and image prompts should be generated from explicit thumbnail prompt input.
- Keep thumbnail direction suspenseful, clear, and relevant without misleading clickbait imagery.
